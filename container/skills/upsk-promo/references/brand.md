## Visual Identity
- **Theme:** Terminal/CLI aesthetic. Dark. Minimal. Technical.
- **Font:** JetBrains Mono (monospace only — no sans-serif anywhere)
- **Colors:** Pure black bg, pure white text, no accent colors
- **Elements:** Blinking cursors, typing effects, scanlines, code blocks
- **No gradients, no rounded corners, no color splashes**
- **Think:** A senior engineer's terminal at 2am. Clean. Focused. No fluff.

## Value Props (in priority order)
1. **Adaptive teaching** — AI instructor remembers how you learn across sessions
2. **Evidence-based scoring** — 5 dimensions scored per module, backed by what you actually did
3. **Zero setup** — One prompt in Codex/Claude Code/Cursor, it handles everything
4. **Real engineering problems** — Not quizzes. You build, break, fix, and ship.
5. **Proof of thinking** — Employers see how you think under pressure, not just scores
6. **Free to start** — 6 skills, first pack of each is free

## Target Audiences

### Students (primary)
- Engineering students who want to stand out
- Juniors who want to prove they can think, not just code
- Self-learners tired of passive video courses
- Pain: "I know DSA but can't design systems" / "I don't know how to prove my skills"

### Colleges (B2B)
- Placement officers who need evidence, not just scores
- Department heads wanting scalable adaptive teaching
- Pain: "We can't personalize instruction at scale" / "Our placement data is just GPAs"

### Employers (future)
- Hiring managers who want to see thinking, not just resumes
- Pain: "Interviews are broken" / "Resumes all look the same"

## Voice & Tone
- Technical but accessible
- Direct, no marketing fluff
- Terminal aesthetic extends to copy: terse, precise, command-like
- Show don't tell — use actual teaching transcripts as proof
- Never say "revolutionary", "cutting-edge", "game-changing"
- OK to say: "It works.", "One prompt.", "Evidence, not grades."
