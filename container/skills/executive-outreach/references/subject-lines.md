# Subject Line Patterns

Professional yet curiosity-inducing subject lines for executive outreach.

## Primary Patterns (Ranked)

### 1. Personal + Direct (BEST)
```
[First Name] — quick note on your [role] search
```
**Why it works:** Casual, non-salesy, shows effort. Feels human.

**Examples:**
- Found a match — Ravi Kiran for Director of Engineering
- Found a match — Ravi Kiran for VP AI Platform
- Found a match — Ravi Kiran for Head of AI

### 2. Initiative + Experience
```
[Company]'s [Initiative] + Ravi's AI experience
```
**Why it works:** Shows research, connects dots.

**Examples:**
- Foodsmart's AI acceleration + Ravi's agent experience
- Acme's India expansion + Ravi's team-building experience
- TechCorp's RAG initiative + Ravi's GraphRAG work

### 3. Different Intro
```
Re: [Role] — a different kind of intro
```
**Why it works:** Curiosity, stands out from typical applications.

**Examples:**
- Re: Director of Engineering — a different kind of intro
- Re: AI Lead role — a different kind of intro

### 4. Personal + Direct
```
[First Name], quick note on your [Role] search
```
**Why it works:** Personal, casual, time-respectful.

**Examples:**
- Alan, quick note on your Director search
- Sarah, quick note on your AI Lead hiring

### 5. Attention Hook
```
Your [Initiative/Post/Move] caught my attention
```
**Why it works:** Flattering, shows engagement.

**Examples:**
- Your AI platform buildout caught my attention
- Your recent CTO move caught my attention
- Your post on agent systems caught my attention

## Context-Specific Variations

### New Leadership
```
Building your engineering org? Quick intro
[First Name] — for your new team
```

### Post-Funding
```
Post-Series [X] scaling — Ravi might help
Scaling after [Company]'s raise
```

### Tech Challenge
```
On [Challenge] — someone who's solved it
Re: your [Topic] post
```

### India/GCC
```
[Company]'s India buildout — local expertise
For your Hyderabad/Bangalore team
```

## Anti-Patterns (Avoid)

### Too Generic
- ❌ Application for [Role]
- ❌ Interest in [Role] at [Company]
- ❌ [Role] Application — Ravi Kiran

### Too Salesy
- ❌ Perfect candidate for your team!
- ❌ You won't want to miss this
- ❌ The candidate you've been looking for

### Too Casual
- ❌ Hey!
- ❌ Quick question
- ❌ Got a minute?

### Too Long
- ❌ Introducing Ravi Kiran — VP Engineering with 15 years of experience in AI, ML, and distributed systems

## Length Guidelines

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Words | 5-10 |
| Characters | 40-60 |
| Key info | Role OR Company OR Hook |

## Testing Subject Lines

Before finalizing, check:
1. Would this stand out in a busy inbox?
2. Does it create curiosity without being clickbait?
3. Is it professional enough for a CTO/CEO?
4. Does it hint at value without overselling?
