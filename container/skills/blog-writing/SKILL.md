# Blog Writing

Write engaging, skimmable long-form content that keeps readers hooked from headline to closing.

## Core Principles

### 1. Start With Purpose

Before writing, answer:
- What should the reader think, feel, or do after reading?
- Who is this actually for?

Strong purpose keeps writing focused and words flow easily.

### 2. Nail the Title and Hook

**Great titles are:**
- Specific, not generic
- Curiosity-sparking
- Promise clear value

**Bad:** "Tips for Better Productivity in 2026"  
**Good:** "Why 95% of Productivity Advice Fails"

**First sentence matters.** Hook the reader immediately. Ease them into your story.

### 3. Structure for Skimmability

People skim before they read. Especially on mobile.

**Make value impossible to miss:**
- Short paragraphs (2-4 lines max)
- Subheadings every 3-5 paragraphs
- Bullets and lists > walls of text
- **Bold the key insight** in almost every section
- One idea per paragraph

### 4. Write Like You Talk

Consistency in tone helps readers feel like they "know" you.

**Too "professional" = boring.** Write like talking to a good friend, not a lecture hall.

**Use:** "you," "your," conversational language

**Bad:** "Successful people always prioritize their tasks."  
**Good:** "You need to stop doing everything—focus on the 3 things that actually move the needle."

### 5. Show, Don't Just Tell

Facts need stories to stick.

For every claim, follow with:
- Personal story
- Before/after
- Specific numbers
- Real example

### 6. Edit Ruthlessly

First draft is for you. Second draft is for the reader.

**After writing:**
- Cut 20-30% of word count
- Remove fillers: "very," "really," "in order to"
- Read aloud—awkward sentences jump out
- Check every claim is verified

### 7. Format for Engagement

- Break up text with whitespace
- Use headers, spacing, visual variety
- End with energy

### 8. Close Strong

Don't fade out.

**End with:**
- Summary of key points
- Question to spark replies
- Call to action
- Challenge to try one tip right now

## Writing Workflow

### Step 1: Define Purpose (2 minutes)
```
Topic: ___
Reader: ___
One thing they should remember: ___
```

### Step 2: Draft Headline Options (3 minutes)
Write 5 variations. Pick the most specific + curiosity-driving.

### Step 3: Outline Structure (5 minutes)
- Hook/intro
- 3-5 sections with subheadings
- Bold insight per section
- Closing CTA

### Step 4: Write Fast (20-30 minutes)
Don't edit. Get words down. Imperfect is fine.

### Step 5: Ruthless Edit (15-20 minutes)
- Cut 30%
- Bold key insights
- Shorten paragraphs
- Verify all facts
- Read aloud

### Step 6: Final Polish (5 minutes)
- Check mobile preview
- Verify subheadings make sense alone
- Ensure CTA is clear

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Generic title | Make specific, add numbers or surprising claim |
| Long paragraphs | Break into 2-3 line chunks |
| No bold insights | Add **key takeaway** every section |
| Weak close | End with question or direct CTA |
| Unverified facts | Check every claim, use "I" only for personal experience |
| Too formal | Replace "one should" with "you should" |

## Quick Checks Before Publishing

- [ ] Title is specific and curiosity-driving
- [ ] First paragraph hooks immediately
- [ ] No paragraph exceeds 4 lines
- [ ] Every section has a bold insight
- [ ] 30% shorter than first draft
- [ ] All facts verified
- [ ] Ends with question or CTA
- [ ] Read aloud, sounds natural

## Examples of Strong Hooks

**Bad:** "In this article, I'll discuss OpenClaw's features."  
**Good:** "I've been running OpenClaw since November. Not as a demo. As my actual infrastructure."

**Bad:** "AI assistants are becoming more popular."  
**Good:** "There's a moment every developer recognizes: you open your `.bashrc` and see `# added 2019 - not sure if needed`."

## Summary

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Title | Specific, curiosity-driving, promises value |
| Hook | Immediate, personal, engaging |
| Paragraphs | 2-4 lines max |
| Subheadings | Every 3-5 paragraphs |
| Key insights | **Bold** in every section |
| Tone | Conversational, "you" focused |
| Evidence | Personal story or specific example per claim |
| Edit | Cut 20-30%, remove fillers |
| Close | Energy, question or CTA |

**Final rule:** If it bores you, it bores them. Cut it.
